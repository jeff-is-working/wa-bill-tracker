⚠️ Policy Committee Cutoff Has Passed — Some Bills Are No Longer Active

What is a legislative cutoff?

The Washington State Legislature has deadlines called "cutoffs" during each session. These deadlines decide when bills must reach certain stages to stay alive.

What happened on February 4th?

The first major cutoff was the Policy Committee Cutoff. Bills that were still waiting to be reviewed by a policy committee in their starting chamber (House or Senate) missed this deadline. These bills can no longer move forward this year.

What does this mean?

  • Active bills have passed their policy committee and are still being considered.
  • Inactive bills missed the cutoff and won't become law this session.
  • Scroll down to see which bills missed the cutoff and are no longer active.

More cutoffs are coming

There are more cutoff dates ahead. Bills must keep moving through the process, or they will miss future deadlines too. Check the cutoff banner above for the next deadline.
